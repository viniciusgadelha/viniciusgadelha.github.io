About me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (CITCEA-UPC), working on advanced solutions for modern power systems and the energy transition. My research focuses on integrating machine learning, optimization, and power flow analysis to improve grid flexibility, reliability, and operational efficiency. During my PhD in Electrical Engineering at UPC, I worked on the impact of power electronics in distribution networks and on new grid concepts based on voltage source converters. I combine data-driven methods with physical grid modeling to ensure both analytical robustness and practical applicability.

My work includes developing digital twins of distribution grids using GIS data, probabilistic power flow frameworks, and machine learning models for congestion forecasting and flexibility management. I collaborate closely with Distribution System Operators and contribute to European R&D projects, translating complex technical results into structured, actionable insights. More broadly, I am interested in building intelligent, data-driven grid architectures that support renewable integration and accelerate a resilient and sustainable energy future.

What i'm doing

  • Machine Learning

    Developing machine learning models that incorporate the physical laws governing power systems to improve accuracy and reliability.

  • Mathematical Optimization

    Optimal power flow formulation of hybrid AC-DC electrical grids interfaced by VSC converters using SOC relaxation.

  • Digital Twins and Grid Modelling

    Development of digital twins of distribution grids based on GIS topology data. Data analysis and management from smart meters and power quality analyzers, including validation, cleaning and troubleshooting of data inconsistencies.

  • Leadership

    Leading and coordinating high-performing research teams.
